I am not going to debate a pandemic - about the bodies of others (and there are many) in a games forum. I know that teamwork, for those who know game development (I am one I know because I study this) is essential, something like 'the air you breathe'. And yet, the game's development continued, under strict conditions that I can't imagine.

Even so, after the game's launch - like any game in this medium - with bigger and / or smaller problems - they released two more multiplatform patches, not to mention the drivers that nvidia itself launched or Sony - incompatible not with Cyberpunk, but with the DirectX 12, alias, all incompatible among all. I am sorry if the world is not that thing between 'good and bad', where we can go out and deposit our frustrations with something that does not serve us, at will. Until today, Sony has not developed the PS5, its final product that still needs polishing. The RTX 3k series show hardware and software problems, technologies like Ray Tracing need more time to be developed ... AMD, too...etc.

Before it was just a few bugs in the game engine, it is not, the problem that Cyberpunk encounters is something that comes up against other problems that already exist and accumulate. But, why? Because Cyber took game development technology to the extreme - and that's not a bad thing, it's good, but it encountered hardware problems that already existed (in relation to bookstores like DirectX 12, Vulkan, etc.). Even in this, even in problems, Cyber is revolutionary in a good way.

So they are all committed to fixing the game, and are together in this job.

just a tip: im playing Cyber in a i5, last generation of this... with a GTX 970, all in high... i have 150 hours in game, and 1 single crash, only (after install). I have ZERO graphic insues... a game like Cyberpunk, its a true work of art, even in engineering, despisting small and common bugs in any game.
