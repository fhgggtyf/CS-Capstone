If I may play devil advocate here for a moment.
A mod is usually created by a person, or small team to make something they want (or their folowing has requested)
It targets one specific element that is desired 'changed'.
And maybe more importantly, if a mod no longer works the common response is: "Its a mod, so that is to be expected." or "Its a stupid mod, fail ..."
But if the official developer screws up with the exact same element that was going to change, the world is too small as we have clear evidence right here these forums.
So a CDPR needs to thread very carefully with EVERYTHING they bring out in the world because the backlash is 10 times larger when it isn't
And that usually mean, testing, changing, testing again, official channel reporting within the organisation, etcetcetec...
This usually increases amount of time needed by a great amount.

And I presume I need not explain any further on what thi means.
