Here a thing about immersion.

When V angers common folks, they begin shooting and more often bashing. Beefy Vs can just stand there, do nothing, and take no damage from it / heal faster than they are harmed.
The citizen doing the bashing just keeps on and on.

More natural behavior for a citizen would be for them to realize that something very weird is going on and start running. After all, everyone's had their share of bashing after a while, and especially if the basher is still able to actually do it, V couldn't have harmed them all that much. A human will get tired of it, and scared to the core if it seems to have no effect.

Another thing that really leaves a gap is that no one attempts to guilt V to weaken them.

In many titles, foes take the appearance of the protagonist's fallen friends and family, and then blame the prot for their demise, how they left them alone, how they are selfish, and so on.

V loses Jackie. He is the perfect candidate for some group to assume his identity and confront V about letting him die and leave on the table the thought that V should die too, before launching their ambush. Not that it would ever work, but it's a powerful psychological tool to weaken one's focus. Shame that it doesn't happen in Cyberpunk 2077.

One sibling of that scenario is understandably a taboo, which I've only seen in Wolfenstein series, when Deathshead makes the prot choose which of his two friends is going to die, and then the dead one becomes the cyborg to fight the prot at the end.
