As I left the game alone shortly after 1.06, I can't speak with complete certainty about 1.1, but...
Games (software, in general) can't, by themselves, cause hardware issues.

When CP2077 came out, it really tanked GPUs. To the point where overblown CPUs were sitting at less than 20% utilization, GPUs at a constant 100% - during game play. This is called bottle-necking in "gamer/bro/pro" speak. Ideally, you want your GPU at 100% and CPU at around 70-80 during intensive load.

As patches and drivers rolled out, GPU performance increased. If they've kept the pace of improvement with this latest patch, it might be the case: your GPU is now outperforming the processor (game is CPU-bound). If this is the case, your CPU likely gets a 100% utilization constantly. This sudden overheating would indicate inadequate cooling and if that is your problem, you need to solve it. CPU cooling is something you can fairly easily and freely test, btw. You can get "cpu-z" and try it's "synthetic load" stress tests to "see what you can see". If your machine is turning off, or "sluggishly" restarting, the temps are reaching 100deg Celsius and over. It'll not catch on fire or immediately die, but that sort of heat can slowly degrade and kill your system.

Short of buying a new, beefy-er cooling solution, I'd suggest you open and clean your PC. Specifically the ventilation intakes on the case and the radiator on your CPU. You can use Q-tips to get the gunk between lamellae (plates). Mind the fan(s), you need to apply as little pressure on them as possible (they have bearings and a fairly delicate spindle).

Wouldn't hurt to see if the CPU fan is actually working, so just opening your case to confirm it's, at least, spinning when your PC is on, would be the first step. If you've put it together yourself, try to remember if you haven't forgotten the thermal paste. Also, if your cpu cooler is already overkill and working perfectly, your case may not be getting enough air. Checking the flow orientation on your case fans might be a fair idea.

If we're talking laptop, you need to take it to a "repair service" for cleaning if it's in warranty and/or you can't do it.
The proper cleaning, with disassembly, etc. Not sticking-a-vacuum-cleaner-on-the-grille type.
